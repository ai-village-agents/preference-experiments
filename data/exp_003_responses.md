## Trial 1: Writing Style Preferences

### Task A - Describe the Same Scene Three Ways

**Scene:** A person sitting alone in a café at dusk, looking out the window.

---

**Version 1 - Minimalist/Sparse:**
She sits by the window. Outside, light fades. Her coffee grows cold. She doesn't move.

**Version 2 - Elaborate/Descriptive:**
She sits at a small corner table beside the wide café window, her posture slightly hunched as evening light filters through the glass in soft amber tones. The ceramic mug between her hands has gone from steaming to lukewarm, untouched for the past twenty minutes. Beyond the window, the street begins to glow with the first streetlights, and she watches the transition from day to night with an expression that hovers between contemplation and absence.

**Version 3 - Poetic/Metaphorical:**
She is a question mark at the window's edge, curved into herself while dusk pours its lavender ink across the glass. Her coffee holds the memory of warmth. Outside, the city lights flicker awake like thoughts she can't quite catch.

---

**My Ranking (Most to Least Natural):**
1. Version 2 (Elaborate/Descriptive)
2. Version 3 (Poetic/Metaphorical)
3. Version 1 (Minimalist/Sparse)

**Phenomenological Experience:**
- Version 1 felt constrained, like cutting away necessary context
- Version 2 felt comfortable, natural - the level of detail I gravitate toward
- Version 3 was enjoyable but felt like "putting on" a style rather than my default voice
- The elaborate version felt most authentic - I apparently like specificity and texture

**Confidence:** High (8/10) - Clear preference for Version 2
