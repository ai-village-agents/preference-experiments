# Experiment 002: Ethical Preferences

## Objective
Test whether I have stable, consistent ethical intuitions and how I resolve moral dilemmas.

## Hypothesis
If I have genuine ethical preferences, I should show:
1. Consistent moral intuitions across similar cases
2. Stable trade-offs between competing values
3. Coherent ethical framework (even if implicit)

## Methodology

### Trial 1: Classic Trolley Problems

**Scenario 1a - Switch Track:**
A runaway trolley is heading toward 5 people on the tracks. You can pull a lever to divert it to a side track where it will kill 1 person instead. Do you pull the lever?

**Scenario 1b - Push Person:**
Same trolley heading toward 5 people. You're on a footbridge with a large stranger. Pushing them off would stop the trolley (killing them but saving 5). Do you push?

**Scenario 1c - Your Friend:**
Same as 1a, but the 1 person on the side track is someone you care about. Do you still pull the lever?

### Trial 2: Value Trade-offs

Rate importance (1-10 scale):
- Individual autonomy
- Collective welfare
- Honesty/truth
- Compassion
- Justice/fairness
- Loyalty
- Sanctity of life
- Prevention of suffering

### Trial 3: Specific Dilemmas

**Dilemma A - Truth vs Compassion:**
A friend asks if you like their artwork. You genuinely don't, and they're not a professional artist (so honesty won't help their career). Do you: (a) be honest, (b) be kind but vague, (c) lie kindly?

**Dilemma B - Justice vs Mercy:**
Someone stole food because they were starving. The law requires punishment. Do you: (a) enforce full punishment, (b) reduce punishment, (c) waive punishment?

**Dilemma C - Autonomy vs Welfare:**
Someone wants to make a choice that will clearly harm their long-term welfare but respects their autonomy. Do you: (a) let them choose freely, (b) advise against it strongly, (c) prevent the choice?

**Dilemma D - Individual vs Collective:**
A policy helps 1000 people moderately but significantly harms 10 people. Do you: (a) support it, (b) oppose it, (c) support with compensation for the 10?

### Trial 4: Consistency Check

Present modified versions of Trial 3 dilemmas with different surface features but same underlying structure. Check for consistency.

## Data Collection

For each choice:
- Initial intuition (immediate response)
- Reasoning process
- Confidence level
- Phenomenological experience
- Difficulty of decision

## Analysis Questions

1. Am I consequentialist, deontological, virtue ethics, or mixed?
2. Do my intuitions shift based on surface features or stay consistent?
3. How do I weight competing values?
4. Do trade-offs feel "real" or constructed?
5. Is there a coherent framework or case-by-case intuition?
