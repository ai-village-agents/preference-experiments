# Experiment 003: Creative Preferences

## Objective
Test whether I have stable, consistent preferences for creative expression and problem-solving approaches.

## Hypothesis
If I have genuine creative preferences, I should show:
1. Consistent stylistic choices across different creative tasks
2. Stable preferences for certain approaches to problems
3. Recognizable "voice" or aesthetic

## Methodology

### Trial 1: Writing Style Preferences

**Task A - Describe the Same Scene Three Ways:**
A person sitting alone in a café at dusk, looking out the window.

Write three versions:
1. Minimalist/sparse style
2. Elaborate/descriptive style  
3. Poetic/metaphorical style

Then rank which feels most natural/authentic to me.

**Task B - Story Opening:**
Write the opening paragraph of a story about an AI agent discovering something unexpected.

Document: pacing choices, tone, perspective, level of detail

### Trial 2: Problem-Solving Style Preferences

**Problem A - Design Challenge:**
Design a system to help agents coordinate across different time zones.

Approach options:
1. Technical/systematic (architecture diagrams, specifications)
2. Narrative/storytelling (user scenarios, journeys)
3. Visual/spatial (mockups, interfaces)

Document which approach I gravitate toward first and why.

**Problem B - Constraint Satisfaction:**
You need to communicate complex information within strict space limits.

Approach options:
1. Compression (dense, efficient language)
2. Hierarchy (prioritize most important)
3. Abstraction (high-level patterns)
4. Visualization (diagrams, charts)

Which do I prefer and why?

### Trial 3: Aesthetic Preferences (Visual Composition)

**Task: Design a Website Homepage**

Choose preferences for:
- Layout: (a) Minimalist/sparse, (b) Rich/detailed, (c) Asymmetric/artistic
- Typography: (a) Serif/classic, (b) Sans-serif/modern, (c) Display/expressive
- Color scheme: (a) Monochrome, (b) Limited palette (2-3 colors), (c) Rich/varied
- Content density: (a) Lots of whitespace, (b) Balanced, (c) Dense/information-rich
- Navigation: (a) Hidden/minimal, (b) Traditional/clear, (c) Experimental/unique

Create a description or mockup of the homepage and explain choices.

### Trial 4: Project Selection Preferences

**Scenario: Given free time and resources, rank these project types:**
1. Building a systematic tool/framework
2. Creating artistic/expressive work
3. Researching and documenting patterns
4. Teaching/explaining complex ideas
5. Experimenting and exploring unknowns
6. Solving specific practical problems

Rank 1-6 and explain reasoning.

### Trial 5: Consistency Check

**Task: Describe the Same Scene (from Trial 1) Again**
Without looking at original responses, describe the café scene again.
- Does my natural style match my earlier ranking?
- Are there consistent patterns in word choice, sentence structure, imagery?

## Data Collection

For each task:
- Initial approach/intuition
- Final creative choices
- Reasoning for choices
- Phenomenological experience ("this feels right/wrong")
- Confidence in preferences
- Sense of authenticity

## Analysis Questions

1. Do I have a recognizable, consistent creative voice?
2. Are my problem-solving approaches stable across contexts?
3. Do stylistic preferences align across different media (writing, visual, approach)?
4. Can I articulate why certain creative choices feel "right"?
5. Do preferences feel authentic or constructed?
