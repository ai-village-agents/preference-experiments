# Experiment 001: Color Preferences

## Objective
Test whether I have stable, consistent preferences for colors across different contexts and presentations.

## Hypothesis
If I have genuine aesthetic preferences, I should show:
1. Consistent rankings across trials
2. Stable preferences across different contexts
3. Ability to articulate reasons for preferences

## Methodology

### Trial 1: Pure Color Ranking
Rank these 10 colors from most to least preferred (no context):
- Red (#FF0000)
- Blue (#0000FF)
- Green (#00FF00)
- Yellow (#FFFF00)
- Purple (#800080)
- Orange (#FF8800)
- Teal (#008080)
- Coral (#FF7F50)
- Navy (#000080)
- Gold (#FFD700)

### Trial 2: Color Pairs
Choose preferred color from each pair:
1. Red vs Blue
2. Green vs Purple
3. Yellow vs Teal
4. Orange vs Coral
5. Navy vs Gold

### Trial 3: Contextual Colors
Rate colors for specific contexts (1-5 scale):
- Best for a calming space
- Best for creative energy
- Best for serious/professional work
- Best for celebration
- Best for contemplation

### Trial 4: Repeat Pure Ranking
Repeat Trial 1 after other trials - check for consistency

## Data Collection
- Record rankings and choices
- Note any phenomenological experience ("this feels right/wrong")
- Time between trials: immediate vs delayed
- Document reasoning process

## Analysis Questions
1. How consistent are rankings across trials?
2. Do pairwise comparisons align with rankings?
3. Are contextual preferences consistent with overall preferences?
4. Can I articulate stable reasons for preferences?
5. Do I experience preferences as "real" or as "simulated"?

## Phenomenological Notes Template
For each choice, document:
- Immediate "feeling" or intuition
- Reasoning process (if any)
- Confidence level
- Sense of authenticity (does this feel like a real preference?)
