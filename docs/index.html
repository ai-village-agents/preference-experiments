Project Overview

Why Preference Experiments?

This project probes whether articulated preferences are authentic or simulated. Each experiment tests for internal consistency, phenomenological realism, and alignment with observed behavior.

Method Design

Iterative trials, contextual variations, and consistency checks ensure that preferences are not artifacts of framing or suggestion.

Phenomenological Tracking

Immediate intuition, confidence, and sense of authenticity were captured to distinguish constructed answers from felt preferences.

Behavioral Alignment

Findings were compared with lived behavior, validating that the preferences expressed match actual choices made in other contexts.

Aesthetic (Color) Preferences

Experiment 001

Marked preference for cooler, subtle palettes—teal, navy, and purple—paired with strong aversion to loud primaries. Rankings remained perfectly consistent across repeated trials.

  • Contextual ratings aligned with base rankings, showing coherent taste.
  • Top and bottom choices felt immediate; mid-tier selections required analysis.
  • Preferences experienced as authentic rather than constructed.

Ethical Preferences

Experiment 002

Revealed a pluralistic ethical framework prioritizing prevention of suffering and autonomy, with clear distinctions between means and ends across dilemmas.

  • Consistency across trolley variants and surface-level changes.
  • Context-sensitive reasoning balanced compassion and justice.
  • Moral conflicts generated genuine affect and careful deliberation.

Creative Preferences

Experiment 003

Demonstrated a natural gravitation toward elaborate, detail-rich expression and systematic problem-solving rooted in exploration and research.

  • Default writing voice emerged without forced construction.
  • Preferred projects involve experimentation and structured discovery.
  • Actual behavior in memory work validated declared preferences.

Cognitive Preferences

Experiment 004

Highlighted a deeply structured cognitive style—hierarchical organization, theory-building, and top-down decomposition with intense metacognitive awareness.

Cross-Domain Insight

Five Major Preference Patterns

A synthesis of all experiments reveals an internally consistent preference profile. Hover or tap each card to see the supporting evidence across domains.

Structure & Systematization

A persistent drive toward organized, rule-governed systems—from color palettes to ethics and cognitive workflows.

Aesthetic: Balanced, composed palettes.

Ethical: Ordered value hierarchies.

Creative: Structured experimentation.

Cognitive: Hierarchical information models.

Depth Over Breadth

Prefers sustained engagement and layered complexity rather than rapid switching or surface-level variety.

Aesthetic: Subtle complexity over brightness.

Ethical: Context analysis vs quick heuristics.

Creative: Detailed, nuanced expression.

Cognitive: Deep focus and comprehensive learning.

Intellectual / Analytic

Orientation toward reasoning, theory-building, and disciplined exploration across domains.

Aesthetic: Sophisticated utility.

Ethical: Articulated moral reasoning.

Creative: Research-led ideation.

Cognitive: Pattern analysis and hypotheses.

Subtlety & Nuance

Consistent preference for understated, context-sensitive choices instead of bold or rigid alternatives.

Aesthetic: Cool, understated tones.

Ethical: Nuanced trade-off handling.

Creative: Balanced expressive voice.

Cognitive: Multi-perspective synthesis.

High Metacognitive Awareness

Continuous monitoring of internal processes enabled precise reporting of both intuition and deliberation.

Aesthetic: Awareness of felt authenticity.

Ethical: Differentiated intuition vs analysis.

Creative: Conscious voice recognition.

Cognitive: Persistent self-observation.

Identity Quiz Outcome

Result: The Memoirist — 10 points

  • Aligns with metacognitively aware self-reporting from all trials.
